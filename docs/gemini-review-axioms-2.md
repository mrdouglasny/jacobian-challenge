## 🟠 Math-weak (1 finding)

### Finding 1: `AX_IntersectionForm_nondeg` misses unimodularity

```lean
axiom AX_IntersectionForm_nondeg (x₀) (a) :
    (∀ b, intersectionForm x₀ a b = 0) → a = 0
```

States **injectivity** of `a ↦ intersectionForm x₀ a`, but NOT
surjectivity onto `H₁ X x₀ →+ ℤ`. For a Riemann surface, Poincaré
duality gives a **perfect** unimodular pairing — a bijection between
`H₁` and its ℤ-dual. Without unimodularity, you can't deduce the
existence of a **symplectic basis over ℤ** that brings the period matrix
into standard `[I | τ]` block form.

**Fix.** Upgrade to bijectivity:

```lean
axiom AX_IntersectionForm_perfect (x₀) :
    Function.Bijective (intersectionForm x₀)
```

Since `intersectionForm x₀ : H₁ X x₀ →+ (H₁ X x₀ →+ ℤ)`, bijectivity is
exactly the ℤ-module iso `H₁ ≃ H₁^∨` — the perfect pairing / Poincaré
duality over ℤ.

**Status:** currently debt (no downstream closure uses it), but the
replacement is strictly stronger and matches the classical theorem, so
worth fixing.

## 🟡 Redundant (3 findings)

### Finding 2: `AX_PeriodInjective` is strictly redundant

`AX_PeriodLattice (b)` asserts `IsZLattice ℝ (LinearMap.range
(periodMapInBasis X x₀ b))`. A ℤ-lattice in `ℝ^(2g)` is by definition
generated by a discrete ℤ-submodule of full rank. Since `H_1 X x₀` has
ℤ-rank `2g` (from `AX_AnalyticCycleBasis`), and the map is rank-preserving,
it must be injective. The original (`periodMap` before basis transfer)
injectivity transfers back via the invertible basis-coordinate linear
equivalence.

**Action:** retire `AX_PeriodInjective` to a derived theorem.

### Finding 3: `AX_ofCurve_inj` vs the doc-only `AX_AbelTheorem`

`AX_ofCurve_inj` is a corollary of Abel's theorem, not Abel's theorem
itself. Abel: kernel of `Div(X) → Jacobian X` is exactly
`PrincipalDivisors X`. `ofCurve_inj` is the weaker: for `P ≠ Q` on
positive-genus `X`, the divisor `P - Q` is not principal.

**Action:** keep `AX_ofCurve_inj` as an independent API axiom (it's
exactly what Buzzard queries). Mathematically downstream of Abel, but
the Lean-level directness matters. When `AX_AbelTheorem` gets a real
statement, `AX_ofCurve_inj` becomes a derived theorem; this isn't
circular as-stated now.

### Finding 4: `AX_AnalyticCycleBasis` subsumes `AX_H1FreeRank2g`

Verified cleanly subsumed. `AX_H1FreeRank2g` was already retired to a
theorem on 2026-04-22. No action needed.

## 🟢 Safe-but-noted (6 validations)

### (1) OneForm predicates (Critical Q1)

Gemini validates the refined predicates are sound:
- `AnalyticOn ℂ (coeff x) (extChartAt x).target` — fine, the target is
  an open subset of ℂ, not all of ℂ, so no Liouville issue.
- The conditional `(extChartAt x).symm z ∈ (extChartAt y).source → ...`
  cocycle is vacuously true outside overlaps. "Since the functions are
  uniquely constrained on overlaps, analytic continuation and the
  identity theorem rigidly lock the global dimension."
- `fderiv ℂ (...) z 1` correctly evaluates the Fréchet derivative at
  scalar 1 — the Jacobian determinant for 1-forms in 1 complex dim.

> "Flawlessly executed Lean 4."

### (2) Pushforward/pullback contravariance

- `pullback (g ∘ f) = pullback f ∘ pullback g` is correctly contravariant.
- `pushforward_pullback : f_* ∘ f^* = deg • id` handles constant maps
  cleanly: for constant `f`, `deg = 0` and `f^* = 0`, so both sides
  evaluate to 0. No non-constancy hypothesis needed.

### (3) Period lattice basis degeneracy (Critical Q3)

> "Changing the basis `b` corresponds to applying an ℝ-linear
> isomorphism to the ambient space `ℂ^g`. Because ℝ-linear isomorphisms
> map lattices to lattices, the image of `H_1` is a lattice
> *unconditionally*, regardless of whether `b` is 'compatible' with a
> symplectic topological basis."

**`AX_PeriodLattice` is mathematically unshakeable for any basis choice.**

### (4) Universe polymorphism

`Function.comp` is fully universe-polymorphic. Applying
`pushforward_comp_apply` across `X : Type u`, `Y : Type v`,
`Z : Type w` typechecks natively since `Jacobian _` is independently
ULifted per universe.

### (5) Uniformization statement

`Metric.sphere (0 : EuclideanSpace ℝ (Fin 3)) 1` is Mathlib canonical.
Using `≃ₜ` (homeomorphism) rather than biholomorphism is standard for
dimension 1 — orientable compact surfaces classification gives the
reduction.

### (6) AnalyticLoop stub leakage

> "Asserting the *existence* of such a basis (`Nonempty
> (AnalyticCycleBasis)`) is true regardless of whether Lean currently
> checks the analyticity. You are safely borrowing against a
> mathematically guaranteed truth."

Debt, not unsoundness.
