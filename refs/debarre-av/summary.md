# Debarre — Two or Three Things I Know About Abelian Varieties

**Citation.** O. Debarre, "Two or Three Things I Know About Abelian Varieties," informal mini-course notes, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, April 17 & 19, 2017. ~15 pages.

**URL.** https://www.math.ens.psl.eu/~debarre/AV.pdf (freely available on Debarre's ENS page).

**Scope.** Short, mostly proof-free survey of classical abelian-variety theory plus a catalogue of examples of principally polarized abelian varieties (PPAVs): Jacobians, Prym varieties, intermediate Jacobians.

## Structure

| § | Topic |

| 2 | Where does one find abelian varieties? (complex tori, Jacobians of smooth projective curves, Picard varieties, Albanese varieties) |

| 5 | Prym varieties |
| 6 | Intermediate Jacobians |
| 7 | Minimal cohomology classes |

## Relevance to the Jacobian challenge

**Moderate — best used as a roadmap / mental model.** Too short to be a working reference, but §2.1–2.2 give concise statements of:

- Complex torus as `V / Λ` with `V` a complex vector space and `Λ` a lattice of full real rank `2 dim V`
- Jacobian of a smooth projective curve `C` as `H⁰(C, Ω¹_C)ᵛ / H_1(C, ℤ)` via the period pairing `(γ, ω) ↦ ∫_γ ω`
- The polarization coming from the intersection form on `H_1`
- Abel-Jacobi / Abel's theorem

These are *exactly* the defining identities we need for the period-lattice construction (basis-free form). §2.1–2.2 is ~3 pages and crystallizes what we're aiming at.

**What it does not have.** Proofs. For those, fall through to Milne (algebraic side) or Mumford / Birkenhake-Lange (complex-analytic side).
